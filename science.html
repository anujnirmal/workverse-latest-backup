TEACHING THROUGH STORYTELLING:

Be it the training or the testing- the entire program journey is delivered through the storyline of an employee working in a startup.

“People remember things better in the same context that they originally learned the information.”

- The context effect, Principle of Behavioural Economics

The continuity that this storyline brings immerses the learner and logically strings together the experience of learning 10 VERY diverse workplace skills in a harmonic flow.

“Stories are about 22 times more memorable than facts alone” by engaging our emotions and making our brains work differently (“transportation”).

- Jerome Bruner
(Harvard University, New York University, University of Oxford)

Immersive and gamified learning

Immersive and gamified learning
Why does learning have to be boring?

Our course simulates a 21st-century workplace. The moment 'learners' join us, they become 'employees.' They work with a 'boss', collaborate with 'colleagues', and deliver outcomes on real-world business goals.

The crux of the program is based on the application of workplace skills supplemented by tasks and challenges. Creating friendly competition amongst learners and their teams and rewarding their progress further incentivizes growth.

I think what I like about gamification and applying the concept of games in learning is that learning through gamification allows us to be more human, it allows us to say that I have autonomy, I can make choices, it allows us to explore, it allows us to collaborate as a team or compete as competitors, which is a motivational trigger for a lot of people.

Mun Choong Lam
(Founder and CEO,Selfdrvn Enterprise)

Gamification to me is like comparing colour television to the radio, basically, it's giving the same content and information but in a more interesting way. If the learners are presented with content that is more engaging, the learner walks away with more.

Matthew Steele
(Affiliate Assistant Professor, Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation)

MEASUREMENT OF INTELLIGENCE:
Can you accurately judge one's professional potential by only meeting them once?
Probably not!

Our 360-degree assessment model tests learners through diverse interventions:

  • The modules put learners in complex workplace situations where they actively use decision-making to figure out the best route.
  • The individual and group Tasks involve creative application of workplace skills.
  • Unscheduled challenges stimulate the uncertainty of a workplace and test a learner's promptness.

Throughout the journey, the learner’s performance is analyzed by our algorithm to finally produce a unique and personalized “Workplace Intelligence Profile” Report. This also provides employers with highly nuanced data, enabling them to make hiring decisions most suited to their needs.
